CBSE Class 12 English Notice Writing: Format, Common Mistakes & Self-Check Guide

A complete, exam-focused breakdown of how to write a full-marks Notice for CBSE Class 12 English — updated for the current marking scheme.

What is Notice Writing?

A Notice is a formal, brief piece of writing used to inform a specific group of people — students, staff, or the public — about an event, announcement, or requirement. In the CBSE Class 12 English exam, Notice Writing appears in the Writing Skills section and typically carries 5 marks, split between content and format.

The Correct Notice Format

Every CBSE Notice must include these elements, in this order:

[NAME OF ISSUING ORGANIZATION] NOTICE [Date] [HEADING — clearly states the subject] [Body: 3-4 sentences covering what, why, when, where, who to contact] [Your Name] [Designation]

The entire Notice should be enclosed in a box (draw a rectangle around it in your answer sheet), and kept within the prescribed word limit — usually 50 words.

Common Mistakes That Cost Marks

Missing the box. Examiners specifically check for a drawn box around the Notice. Forgetting this costs format marks even if your content is perfect.
Vague heading. A heading like "Notice" alone isn't enough — it must state the actual subject, e.g. "Annual Sports Day — Notice for Participation."
Missing the issuing authority's name and designation. Every Notice needs a clear "who is this from" at the bottom — name and designation/role.
Exceeding or falling short of the word limit. Both cost marks. Aim to land within 5 words of the limit either side.
Missing the date. Every Notice needs a date, usually placed just below the organization name.
Writing in an informal tone. A Notice should read like an official announcement, not a casual message — avoid contractions and conversational phrasing.

Self-Check Checklist

Before finishing, run through this list:

Organization name is at the top
The word "NOTICE" appears, centered or clearly visible
Date is included
Heading clearly states the specific subject
Body answers what, when, where, and who to contact
Word count is within the prescribed limit
Name and designation of the issuing person appear at the end
The entire notice is enclosed in a box
Tone is formal throughout

Example Notice

GREENWOOD PUBLIC SCHOOL NOTICE 12 March 2026 Inter-House Debate Competition This is to inform all students of Classes XI and XII that an Inter-House Debate Competition will be held on 20 March 2026 in the school auditorium at 10 a.m. Interested students should register their names with the undersigned by 15 March 2026. Ritika Sharma Cultural Secretary

This example hits all the checklist points: organization name, date, specific heading, clear body, contact details, and a proper sign-off — all within a tight word count.
